13.MAIL SERVICE INJECTION

Mail service injection is a type of security vulnerability that occurs when an attacker injects malicious data into email messages or email services.

Types of Mail Service Injection

1. Email Header Injection: Injecting malicious data into email headers.

2. Email Body Injection: Injecting malicious data into email bodies.

Impact of Mail Service Injection

1. Spam and Phishing: Attackers can send spam or phishing emails.

2. Malware Distribution: Attackers can distribute malware through email.

3. Reputation Damage: Attackers can damage the reputation of the email service or organization.

Prevention Measures

1. Input validation: Validate user input to prevent malicious data injection.

2. Output encoding: Encode output to prevent XSS attacks.

3. Email filtering: Implement email filtering to detect and block malicious emails.

4. Secure email service configuration: Configure email services securely to prevent injection attacks.
